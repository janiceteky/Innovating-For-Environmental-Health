Testing buildings for mold is time‐consuming, expensive, and unreliable. No "safe" levels of mold has been established. The most important actions to stop or prevent mold growth are: keep the building dry, stop leaks, get rid of mold-contaminated items, fix sources of dampness and notify the school administration when problems are not addressed.

Unfortunately inadequate maintenance will allow molds to spread on ceilings, behind and on walls, on floors and carpets, and in books and other water-absorbing materials. Molds are a type of fungus. They eat or digest what they are growing on, and they grow colonies by producing long filaments of cells that form visible fuzzy or slimy patches.

If you see fuzzy, slimy or discolored surfaces, especially in damp or wet areas, it's probably mold. Molds are often green, purple, or orange. An earthy or musty odor, or a smell like alcohol, is often a sign of hidden molds.
